Research Highlights

Viscoelastic Abrasive Finishing Tool Development

Developed polymer-based viscoelastic abrasive finishing tools for improving rough additively manufactured metallic surfaces. The tool combines compliance, abrasive action, and shape adaptability.

Surface Improvement of Additively Manufactured SS316L

Investigated the effect of spindle speed, penetration depth, finishing time, and abrasive conditions on surface roughness reduction. The work focused on reducing AM-induced surface defects such as partially melted particles, stair-stepping, and rough asperities.

Tribology and Surface Integrity

Studied friction, wear, and surface morphology of as-printed and finished stainless steel surfaces. The work helped link surface finishing with functional surface performance.

Tool Life and Wear Analysis

Analysed the degradation of viscoelastic abrasive tools during finishing. The study examined tool wear, material removal, and finishing quality.
